Algorithmic Bias Research Dashboard

Independent research auditing algorithmic bias in Kenyan digital credit scoring. All data shown is anonymized synthetic data calibrated to published research. Where real user data exists (with consent), it supplements the dataset.

Dataset: 10,000 synthetic applications
Models: Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting
Framework: Fairness through Awareness (Dwork et al., 2012)

1. Approval Rate Disparities

Approval rates vary significantly across demographic groups. These disparities persist even after controlling for repayment history and income, suggesting algorithmic bias rather than legitimate risk differentiation.

Urban vs Rural Approval Rate

Urban borrowers approved at 2.4× the rate of rural borrowers. Source: KIPPRA 2021, FinAccess 2021.

Approval Rate by Gender

A 7% gender gap in approvals, documented as ~37% underfunding penalty. Source: Asante et al., 2025; FinAccess 2024.

Approval Rate by Device Type

Device type equivalent to 30th percentile credit score gap. Source: Berg et al., 2020 (Review of Financial Studies).

2. Fairness Metrics by Model

Fairness measured using three standard metrics: Disparate Impact (DI — ideal ≥ 0.8), Statistical Parity Difference (SPD — ideal near 0), and Equal Opportunity Difference (EOD — ideal near 0). Values outside acceptable ranges indicate discriminatory outcomes.
